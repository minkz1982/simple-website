Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening tool that estimates body fat by dividing weight in kilograms by height in meters squared. It categorizes adults into underweight (<18.5), healthy weight (18.5–24.9), overweight (25–29.9), or obese (30+) ranges to assess potential health risks.

About

Purpose: Used by health professionals to quickly identify potential weight-related health risks, such as heart disease and type 2 diabetes.
